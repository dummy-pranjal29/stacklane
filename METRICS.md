**North Star metric**: Total dollars of potential savings identified per week. (This proves the tool is actually delivering value to users, which is a prerequisite for them trusting Credex).

**3 input metrics driving the North Star**:

1. Audits started per week (Top of funnel volume).
2. Form completion rate (Friction measurement: are 8 tools too many to ask about?).
3. High-savings threshold trigger rate (Percentage of audits that uncover >$500/mo in savings).

**What you'd instrument first**:
Drop-off tracking inside the form. I need to know exactly which step (team size, spend amount, or tool selection) causes users to abandon the page.

**What number triggers a pivot**:
If the High-savings threshold trigger rate is consistently below 5% after 200 audits. If almost nobody is overspending by enough to justify a Credex consultation, we are targeting the wrong size of company, and must immediately pivot marketing efforts from "Indie hackers/Startups" to "Enterprise/Series C+".
